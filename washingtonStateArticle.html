5 Spooky Locations in Washington State, USA

Many, many fabulous tales and artworks in the horror, sci-fi and fantasy genre have been spawned in this luscious, green, densely forested state – including Robin Hobb’s famous Realm of the Elderings fantasy series, which is, in my opinion, one of the most psychologically astute and best-written works of fantasy fiction out there. For those unfamiliarised with Terry Miles’ TANIS and RABBITS, or Radiotopia’s Polybius horror podcasts, these audio dramas have basically created a whole mythos of the area. This is alongside Seattle’s notoriety for producing excellent grunge music in the 1990s and still being host to a panoply of cool, alternative venues that veer away from the mainstream.

1. The Hoh National Rainforest

The Hoh National Rainforest is only one of many sublime and magical national parks and forests in the Washington State area. However, it is one of the greenest and the most lush, with creeping, hanging moss, enormous ferns, and cold, fast flowing rivers. It is definitely somewhere to put on your bucket list if you’re a nature lover.

2. MoPOP Museum

Wonderful museum

Seattle’s museum of pop culture, designed by the architectural legend who is Frank Gehry, is kind of a Mecca for anyone interested in the fantasy or horror genres, rock music, or indie games. There are current exhibitions called ‘Fantasy: Worlds of Myth and Magic’, where you can see props and costumes from movies such as The Princess Bride or The Wizard of Oz, and an extensive display called Scared to Death: The Thrill of the Horror Film, that promises to be an immersive and inspiring experience for any horror fan. The Indie Games exhibit is also not to be missed.

3. Underground Seattle

Gloomy and horrifying tunnels

Most of Seattle was destroyed in the 19th century during a great fire, and the city as we know it was built atop the ruins. There are still many underground passages and abandoned rooms which you can explore fully as a tourist attraction.

4. For David Lynch fans: Snoqualmie Falls

Majestic and sublime waterfall

Snoqualmie Falls was where Twin Peaks was set. Under an hour drive from Seattle, you can visit the Twede’s Café that was features in the series and immerse yourself in the atmosphere of this spooky town surrounded by the dense pinewood forest.

5. Blake Island and Tillicum Village

Lluc Monastery

One of the things that fascinates me most about the Pacific Northwest in the USA is the rich history and culture of the American indigenous First Peoples and the traditions of the native tribes. You can take a ferry to this island from the city of Seattle, where Tillicum Village has preserved the customs and traditions of the native peoples. You can explore the totems and the art of these tribes and learn about their cultural significance, to get a complete feel of what America was like before the European’s arrival, an important acknowledgment of the indigenous’ peoples’ civilisation.

6. The Owl 'n' Thistle

With its fitting gothic-inspired name, the Owl ‘n’ Thistle is a dark-academia-themed Irish pub complete with hearty stews and pints of dark Guiness. It is an excellent place to see lots of local bands play gigs.

7. The Haunted Moore Theatre

Sitting atop Seattle’s oldest cemetery, this theatre is rich in history and lore that would lure every aspiring paranormal investigator. Apparently, the abandoned theatre often still smells like cigar smoke, while the basement is a popular site for ghosthunters – there have been many reports of hearing mysterious voices there. It has also been frequently used by horror filmmakers due to its spooky appearance.

8. Ye Olde Curiosity Shop

This combination of a shop and a museum is host to many of the oddities and aberrations found in nature and culture, including pigs with three tails and shrunken heads. There are weird creatures preserved in jars of formaldehyde and all kinds of mummified beings. There will always be so many interesting things to look at or buy here, including Pacific Northwest Native Americans’ hand crafted art and jewelry made out of insects and plants.
