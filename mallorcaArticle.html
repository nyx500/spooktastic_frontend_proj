5 Spooky Locations in Mallorca, Spain

Bellver Castle
Inside Bellver
Bellver in the forest

1. Bellver Castle

Bellver is a beautiful, perfectly preserved Gothic castle: in fact, it is the oldest circular castle in Europe! Despite the fact that it’s also worth a visit because the caste offers majestic and splendid views of the Mallorcan coast and the fragrant, surrounding pine forest, Bellver, an architectural marvel, also has a history rich in tales of ghosts and legends.

2. Es Trenc and Ses Selines Beach Bunkers

In the 1940s, General Franco, the fascist dictator of Spain, was worried about a potential Allied invasion – he therefore built about twenty coastal fortifications, including concrete dugouts and heavy artillery on this gorgeous coastline, that are perfectly preserved yet abandoned, giving a foreboding feeling to otherwise sandy and spotless beaches.

3. The Drach Caves

Mallorca is home to one of the biggest underground lakes in the world, and to many, many constellations of caverns and caves full of impressive stalagmites and stalactites. One of the most impressive cave formations are the Drach caves in Porto Cristo – meaning ‘Caves of the Dragon’. The underground lakes are so vast, that you can even get a boat ride across them – a once in a lifetime experience. In some of Mallorca’s caves, you can attend a classical concert among the stalactites. When I visited the caves, I immediately thought of the Hobbit – you can really imagine a Tolkienesque Gollum or Dragon hiding in these mysterious labyrinths!

4. Explore Sea Caves

As mentioned above, Mallorca is riddled with natural caves. If you are not too scared, you can take this opportunity to go diving in a cave in Cova de coloms. The caves are lined with blood red minerals, which give an eerie appearance. You are provided with all the necessary equipment and do not need to have any prior experience with caving!

5. Lluc Monastery

Lluc Monastery

The Lluc Monastery is the most sacred site on Mallorca. It is an isolated, tranquil monastery with dramatic views high in the mountains. With the surrounding steep cliffs and forests, it is really an example of the aesthetically sublime.
